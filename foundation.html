Curriculum

What You'll Study

20+ structured course modules covering the fundamentals and applied skills of electronics engineering technology.

01

Electrical Safety & Fundamentals

Electrical safety standards and best practices
Units, prefixes, and basic electrical theory
Introduction to voltage, current, and resistance
Ohm's Law and its applications
02

Test & Measurement Tools

Multimeter setup, functions, and proper use
Voltage, current, and resistance measurements
Continuity and diode testing
Reading and interpreting measurement data
03

DC Circuit Theory

Series, parallel, and series-parallel circuits
Kirchhoff's Voltage and Current Laws (KVL, KCL)
Power calculations and energy consumption
Circuit analysis methods and techniques
04

AC Circuit Theory

Sinusoidal waveforms and AC quantities
Capacitors and inductors in AC circuits
Impedance, reactance, and phase angles
Power factor and AC power analysis
05

Electronic Components

Resistors, capacitors, and inductors
Diodes, transistors, and basic semiconductors
Reading component datasheets and specs
Component identification and selection
06

Introduction to Digital Electronics

Binary number systems and Boolean algebra
Logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR
Combinational logic circuits
Introduction to flip-flops and sequential logic
07

Circuit Schematics & Documentation

Reading and interpreting circuit diagrams
Standard electronic symbols and notations
Wiring diagrams vs. schematic diagrams
Technical documentation standards
08

Intro to Automation & Control

Overview of industrial automation systems
Sensors, actuators, and control elements
Basic relay and contactor circuits
Introduction to PLCs and control technology

+ Additional elective modules on renewable energy fundamentals, fiber optics overview, and industrial safety.
